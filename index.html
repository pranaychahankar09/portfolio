Case Study · 01 / 02
Customer Churn Analysis

Project Overview

A comprehensive churn analysis on 7,000+ customer records to identify why customers were leaving, which segments were at highest risk, and what interventions could improve retention.

What I Did

  • Cleaned and preprocessed 7,000+ records using SQL and Advanced Excel — handling nulls, duplicates, and inconsistent formats.
  • Conducted KPI reporting across key metrics: churn rate, revenue risk, customer lifetime value, and cohort retention.
  • Performed EDA and customer segmentation to identify behavioral patterns among churned vs. retained customers.
  • Designed an interactive Power BI dashboard showcasing churn rate, revenue risk, and customer lifecycle metrics.
  • Generated actionable insights and recommendations to strengthen customer retention strategies.

Key Findings

  • Identified top churn drivers: pricing dissatisfaction, low engagement frequency, and support friction.
  • High-risk segment accounted for a disproportionate share of churned revenue despite being a small portion of the base.
  • Month 3–5 of customer lifecycle showed the highest churn probability — critical intervention window identified.

Case Study · 02 / 02
UPI Transaction Performance & Insights Dashboard

Project Overview

A large-scale analysis of 20,000+ UPI transaction records across 4 banks, focused on understanding success/failure rates, regional patterns, and payment behavior to surface operational inefficiencies.

What I Did

  • Examined 20,000+ transaction records across 4 banks to assess success rates (~80%), failure patterns, and payment performance.
  • Developed dynamic dashboards illustrating transaction trends, regional activity, and payment behavior across multiple categories.
  • Performed time-series analysis to detect peak transaction periods and seasonal patterns.
  • Conducted category-wise analysis to surface operational inefficiencies and highlight key improvement areas.
  • Delivered structured reports with actionable recommendations to support data-driven decisions.

Key Findings

  • Overall transaction success rate of ~80%; failure clustering identified in specific time windows and bank pairs.
  • Regional analysis revealed high-activity zones contributing the majority of total transaction volume.
  • Weekend transaction volumes were significantly lower than weekdays, with failure rates inversely higher — suggesting infrastructure scaling gaps.
  • Category-wise breakdown: utility payments had the highest failure rate vs. peer-to-peer transfers.
